Introduction to Urban Agriculture

1. Definition and Scope

What is Urban Agriculture?

Urban agriculture is the practice of cultivating crops, growing food, and raising livestock in urban and peri-urban settings. It includes producing, processing, and distributing agricultural goods within city spaces to address food security, promote sustainable livelihoods, and integrate agriculture into the urban economy.

Key Features
  1. Utilizes Urban Spaces: Urban agriculture makes innovative use of spaces like vacant lots, rooftops, balconies, walls, and even abandoned buildings to grow food and raise animals.
  2. Comprehensive Activities: Beyond farming, it encompasses food processing (turning raw crops into consumable products), distribution (transporting produce to consumers), and marketing.
  3. Scales of Operation: Practices range from small-scale home gardens to large commercial urban farms
Categories
  1. Food Production

    2. Includes cultivating vegetables, fruits, herbs, aquaponics (combining fish farming and hydroponics), and hydroponics (growing plants without soil).

  2. Livestock Production

    3. Small-scale raising of poultry, rabbits, or goats, and even urban beekeeping.

  3. Ornamentals:

    4. Growing flowers and landscaping plants for beautifying spaces, increasing biodiversity, and promoting mental well-being.
